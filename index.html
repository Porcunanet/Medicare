Key Terms Glossary
BeneficiaryA person who has Medicare benefits.
PremiumMonthly amount paid to maintain health insurance coverage.
DeductibleOut-of-pocket amount paid before insurance coverage begins; usually set per calendar year.
CoinsurancePercentage of cost the beneficiary pays after insurance pays (e.g., 20% of a $200 service = $40).
CopaymentFixed dollar amount paid per service or item received (e.g., $15 per doctor visit).
Medically NecessaryThe basis Medicare uses to decide whether to cover a service — must be reasonable, necessary, and within scope.
InpatientFormally admitted to a hospital by an attending physician.
OutpatientNot formally admitted; may stay overnight (e.g., observation status is outpatient care).
Skilled CareMedical care provided by or under the direct supervision of a licensed nurse or therapist.
Personal CareNon-medical care (bathing, dressing, bathroom assistance) provided by a home health aide.
ABNAdvance Beneficiary Notice — informs the patient that Medicare may not pay for a service before they receive it.
MSN / EOBMedicare Summary Notice (Original Medicare) or Explanation of Benefits (MA Plans) — summaries of services and costs; not a bill.

Course 2 Key Terms
Original MedicareThe traditional Medicare program administered directly by the federal government (Parts A and B).
Medicare Advantage (Part C)Private health plans that contract with the federal government to deliver Medicare benefits, usually including Part D coverage.
Part AHospital insurance — covers inpatient hospital stays, SNF care, hospice, and some home health. Usually premium-free with 10+ work years.
Part BMedical insurance — covers outpatient services, doctor visits, preventive care, and durable medical equipment.
Part DPrescription drug coverage — added to Original Medicare or bundled into most Medicare Advantage plans.
SSDISocial Security Disability Insurance — receiving SSDI for 24 months qualifies individuals under 65 for Medicare (ALS exception: immediate).
ESRDEnd-Stage Renal Disease — permanent kidney failure requiring dialysis or transplant; qualifies individuals for Medicare at any age.
MedicaidFederal- and state-funded, state-administered health insurance. Eligibility based on income and assets, not age.
Dual-EligibleIndividuals who qualify for both Medicare and Medicaid. Medicaid may cover Medicare cost-sharing and services Medicare excludes.
CMSCenters for Medicare & Medicaid Services — the federal agency that writes coverage rules and oversees both programs.
SSASocial Security Administration — handles Medicare enrollment, issues Medicare cards, and manages related benefit programs.
Work History / QuartersNumber of years or quarters an individual paid Social Security taxes. Affects Part A premiums but not Medicare eligibility.

Course 3 Key Terms
Medigap (Medicare Supplement)Supplemental insurance that works alongside Original Medicare to pay cost-sharing gaps like coinsurance. Not compatible with Medicare Advantage.
MOOP (Maximum Out-of-Pocket)The annual cap on what a beneficiary pays for covered services. Original Medicare has NO MOOP; all Medicare Advantage plans are required to have one.
Provider NetworkThe group of doctors, hospitals, and providers contracted with a Medicare Advantage plan. Seeing out-of-network providers may cost more or be uncovered.
ReferralAuthorization from a primary care provider to see a specialist. Not required under Original Medicare; may be required under some Medicare Advantage plans (e.g., HMOs).
Stand-alone Part D PlanA separate prescription drug plan purchased alongside Original Medicare. Medicare Advantage plans usually bundle drug coverage, making a separate Part D plan unnecessary.
HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)A type of Medicare Advantage plan that typically requires beneficiaries to use in-network providers and obtain referrals to see specialists.
PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)A type of Medicare Advantage plan that allows use of out-of-network providers (usually at higher cost) and generally does not require referrals.
Medicare CardThe red/white/blue card issued by SSA for Original Medicare beneficiaries. Those in Medicare Advantage use the card from their private plan — using the wrong card causes billing problems.
Extra Benefits (MA Plans)Some Medicare Advantage plans offer benefits beyond Original Medicare such as routine dental, vision, and hearing coverage.
Part B PremiumThe monthly fee all Medicare beneficiaries pay for Part B coverage, regardless of whether they have Original Medicare or Medicare Advantage.
