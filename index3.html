Key Terms
Original Medicare Appeal
A formal request a beneficiary makes to Medicare when they disagree with a coverage or payment decision on a health care service or item they received.
Medicare Summary Notice (MSN)
A document sent to beneficiaries after receiving care that summarizes services, what Medicare paid, and what was denied. Not a bill. Needed to begin a standard appeal.
MAC (Medicare Administrative Contractor)
Reviews Level 1 Redetermination requests. Should issue a decision within 60 days of receiving the appeal.
QIC (Qualified Independent Contractor)
Performs Level 2 Reconsideration. Reviews the MAC's decision and issues a new decision within 60 days (standard) or 72 hours (expedited).
OMHA (Office of Medicare Hearings and Appeals)
Conducts Level 3 hearings with an Administrative Law Judge (ALJ). Beneficiary must meet the amount in controversy requirement to proceed.
QIO (Quality Improvement Organization)
Also called BFCC-QIO. Handles Level 1 redeterminations in expedited appeals. Contacted directly by the beneficiary when care is ending.
Good Cause Extension
Allows a beneficiary to file late at any level of appeal if they have a valid reason (e.g., illness, missing notice, lack of documentation).
Detailed Notice of Discharge
Document from provider explaining why care is ending and what Medicare rules apply. Required before filing an expedited appeal.

Key Terms
Medicare Advantage (MA) Appeal
A formal request a beneficiary makes to their MA Plan when they disagree with the plan's coverage or payment decision on a health care service or item.
Organization Determination
A decision made by a plan or provider acting on behalf of a plan. Must be issued before a beneficiary can begin an appeal. Covers approval, payment, or continuation of care.
IRE (Independent Review Entity)
Reviews the plan's Level 1 reconsideration decision at Level 2. Must decide within 30 days (standard pre-service), 60 days (post-service), or 72 hours (expedited).
Notice of Denial of Medical Coverage
Official denial notice from the MA plan. Instructs the beneficiary on how and when to appeal. Triggers the 60-day window to file a Level 1 appeal.
Explanation of Benefits (EOB)
Statement from the MA plan showing what services were billed, what the plan paid, and what was denied. Can trigger a post-service standard appeal.
BFCC-QIO (Quality Improvement Organization)
Beneficiary and Family-Centered Care QIO. Contacted by beneficiary when hospital or non-hospital care is ending to begin an expedited ending-care appeal.
Standard Appeal
An MA appeal that follows standard timelines: pre-service (haven't received care yet) or post-service (already received care). Both follow the same 5-level process.
Expedited Appeal
An MA appeal with accelerated timelines when a beneficiary's health would be at risk under standard processing. Available for pre-service situations and when care is ending.

Key Terms
Part D Appeal
A formal request a beneficiary makes to their Part D plan if they disagree with the plan's coverage or payment decision on a prescription drug or health care service.
Coverage Determination
The plan's initial decision about whether it will cover a drug. Must be issued before a beneficiary can begin a Part D appeal. Standard: 72 hours. Expedited: 24 hours.
Formulary
The plan's list of covered prescription drugs. If a drug is not on the formulary or is excluded from all Part D coverage, the plan will not cover it.
Notice of Denial of Medicare Prescription Drug Coverage
The official notice from the plan when it denies coverage for a drug. Contains instructions on how and when to appeal. Triggers the 60-day window to file a Level 1 appeal.
Exception Request
A request asking the plan to cover a drug that is not on its formulary, or to lower the copay tier for a drug. Can be standard (72 hrs) or expedited (24 hrs).
IRE (Independent Review Entity)
Reviews the plan's Level 1 redetermination at Level 2. Standard: 7 days. Expedited: 72 hours. Decides whether plan should cover drug or allow a tiering exception.
Standard Part D Appeal
A Part D appeal processed on standard timelines. Level 1 and 2 decisions issued within 7 days. Level 3 and 4 decisions within 90 days. Used when health is not urgently at risk.
Expedited Part D Appeal
A Part D appeal with accelerated timelines when health could be harmed by waiting. Level 1 and 2: 72 hours. Level 3 and 4: 10 days. Beneficiary or doctor can request expedited review.
